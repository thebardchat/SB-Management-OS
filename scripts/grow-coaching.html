G

GOAL

Establish what the team member wants to achieve

Purpose: Define a clear, specific, measurable goal for this coaching session. The goal should be challenging yet achievable.

Key Questions to Ask:

"What would you like to focus on today?"
"What does success look like for you in this area?"
"How will you know when you've achieved this goal?"
"What would achieving this mean to you and your career?"
"Is this goal within your control to achieve?"
💡 Coach Tips
  • Ensure the goal is SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
  • Let the team member define their own goal - don't impose yours
  • Align personal goals with team/company objectives
R

REALITY

Understand the current situation and context

Purpose: Assess where the team member currently stands in relation to their goal. Identify obstacles, resources, and relevant factors.

Key Questions to Ask:

"Where are you now in relation to your goal?"
"What have you tried so far? What happened?"
"What obstacles are you facing?"
"What resources do you have available to you?"
"On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your current performance in this area?"
"What's working well that you can build on?"
💡 Coach Tips
  • Be objective and non-judgmental
  • Use open-ended questions to encourage reflection
  • Help identify blind spots they may not see
O

OPTIONS

Generate possible solutions and strategies

Purpose: Brainstorm all possible options for moving forward. Encourage creative thinking and explore multiple paths to the goal.

Key Questions to Ask:

"What options do you have?"
"What else could you do?"
"If there were no limitations, what would you try?"
"What would someone you admire do in this situation?"
"What are the pros and cons of each option?"
"Which option excites you the most?"
💡 Coach Tips
  • Don't evaluate options too quickly - quantity first
  • Encourage "What if..." thinking
  • Offer your ideas only after they've exhausted theirs
W

WILL / WAY FORWARD

Commit to specific actions and accountability

Purpose: Convert insights into action. Establish specific commitments, timelines, and accountability measures.

Key Questions to Ask:

"Which option will you pursue?"
"What specifically will you do? By when?"
"On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to this action?"
"What might get in the way? How will you handle it?"
"What support do you need from me or others?"
"How should we follow up on your progress?"
💡 Coach Tips
  • If commitment is below 7, revisit the options
  • Schedule the follow-up before ending the session
  • Document the commitments in writing
